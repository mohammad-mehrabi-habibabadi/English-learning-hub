üì¢ What is "Stance"?

In IELTS Task 2, your "stance" is your position or opinion. A common mistake is only stating your opinion in the conclusion. A high-scoring essay has a "clear position throughout the response".

Weak Voice (Band 5) "Pollution is increasing. Cars cause smoke. This is a problem."
Fact-heavy, robotic, no emotion or judgment.
Strong Voice (Band 7) "Unfortunately, pollution is rising rapidly. Cars emit harmful fumes, which is a serious issue."
Words like 'Unfortunately' and 'harmful' show you care about the topic.

2. Attitude Adverbs

Start your sentences with these adverbs to signal your feeling before you even state the fact.

Surprisingly, (Shows something unexpected)
"Surprisingly, many people prefer working from home despite the isolation."
Inevitably, (Shows something cannot be avoided)
"Inevitably, the cost of living will continue to rise."
Crucially, (Shows the most important point)
"Crucially, the government must invest in renewable energy."

üöÄ The Art of Concession

Band 8+ writers don't just ignore the other side. They acknowledge it (Concession) and then destroy it (Refutation). This shows complex critical thinking.

Structure: "While X is true, Y is more important."

Example 1: Technology "Admittedly, automation boosts efficiency. However, the social cost of job losses cannot be ignored."
Admittedly = I accept this point is true.
Example 2: Education "While it is true that university is expensive, the long-term career benefits far outweigh the costs."
"Far outweigh" is a very strong stance marker.

üöÄ Distancing Language

Sometimes you want to mention an opinion that is not yours. You must "distance" yourself from it so the reader knows you don't agree.

Common Phrases:

  • "Proponents of this view claim that..." (Others claim, not me)
  • "It is often argued that..." (General opinion)
  • "Skeptics maintain that..."
"Critics often assert that space exploration is a waste of money. However, I believe it drives innovation."
Using 'assert' instead of 'say' makes it sound more academic.

üöÄ Evaluative Nouns

Instead of saying "This argument is wrong," use nouns that carry judgement. This is extremely high-level style.
