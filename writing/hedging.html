In academic writing (IELTS Task 2), nothing is 100% certain. If you say "Video games cause violence", it is a false statement because not everyone becomes violent.

Instead, we use Hedging (Cautious Language) to make our arguments scientific and bulletproof.

ğŸ› ï¸ The Toolkit

1. Modal Verbs (The most common)

Could / Might / May / Can

Use these instead of "is" or "will".

  • "This solution could be effective."
  • "Problems might arise."

2. Tentative Verbs

Suggest / Appear / Seem / Tend to

Use these to show interpretation or trends.

  • "The evidence suggests that..."
  • "Children tend to learn faster."
  • "It appears to be difficult."

3. Adverbs of Probability

Likely / Possibly / Probably / Frequently
  • "It is highly likely that costs will increase."
  • "This frequently results in failure."

4. Limiting Quantifiers

The majority of / A significant number of / Some / In many cases

Never say "All" or "Everyone". Be specific.

  • "In many cases, diet plays a key role."
  • "A vast number of people agree."

Don't overdo it!
