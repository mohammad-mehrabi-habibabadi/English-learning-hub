Predator (n.)
An animal that hunts other animals for food.
"Lions are top predators in the wild."
Prey (n.)
An animal that is hunted and eaten by another.
"Small birds often become prey for cats."
Mosquitos (n.)
Small flying insects that bite and feed on blood.
"Mosquitos are very annoying in the summer."
Breed (v.)
To produce young animals (babies).
"Many birds return to this island to breed every year."
Migrate (v.)
To move from one region to another according to the seasons.
"Whales migrate thousands of miles to find warmer water."
Fearless (adj.)
Not afraid of anything.
"The honey badger is a fearless animal."
Vicious (adj.)
Violent, cruel, and dangerous.
"Wild dogs can be quite vicious when they hunt."

ğŸŒ Ecology & Conservation

Ecological balance (n.)
A stable state of nature where everything is healthy.
"We need to maintain the ecological balance."
Conservation efforts (n.)
Work done to protect nature and animals.
"Without money, conservation efforts will be futile."
Play an irreplaceable role (phrase)
To have a very important function that nothing else can do.
"Bees play an irreplaceable role in our ecosystem."
Essential resources (n.)
Basic things we need to live (water, air, food).
"Nature provides us with essential resources."
Interdependent (adj.)
Depending on each other.
"In the wild, plants and animals are interdependent."
Preserve diversity (v.)
To protect the variety of life (plants/animals).
"We must act now to preserve diversity in the rainforest."
A domino effect (n.)
When one event causes a chain of other events.
"Loss of habitat can trigger a domino effect."
Futile (adj.)
Useless; having no result.
"Arguments are futile when no one listens."
Environmental degradation (n.)
Damage to the environment (pollution, destruction).
"Air pollution is a major cause of environmental degradation."
For the entire globe (phrase)
For the whole planet/world.
"Climate change is a problem for the entire globe."
Foundation of our survival (phrase)
The basis of our existence.
"The environment is the foundation of our survival."

Buying Time Phrase
Use this when you need a moment to think:
"That is a challenging question to answer. Personally, I believe it is crucial that we protect animals."
Part 1: Introduction (4-5 Minutes)
Q: Do you like watching wildlife documentaries?
"Yes, definitely. I find them really educational. From my perspective, they help us understand how animals behave in their natural habitats. I usually watch them on weekends to relax."

Q: Have you ever been to a zoo or a safari park?
"Yes, I have visited the city zoo a few times. It's a good place to see animals like lions and elephants close up. However, in my view, safari parks are better because animals have more space to move around and aren't kept in small cages."
Part 2: Long Turn (1-2 Minutes)
Describe a wild animal you are interested in.
  • What it looks like
  • Where it lives

Part 3: Discussion (4-5 Minutes)
Q: Why do we need to protect wild animals?
"That is a challenging question to answer, but in my view, it is absolutely essential. The main reason is that all species are interdependent. If we lose one animal, it can trigger a domino effect that hurts the whole ecosystem. For example, without bees, we wouldn't have enough food. So, conservation efforts are basically about safeguarding the foundation of our survival."


Q: What are the main problems causing species extinction?
"There are several factors working simultaneously. The biggest problem is definitely environmental degradation caused by humans. We are destroying forests to build cities, which leaves animals with nowhere to live. Another issue is climate change, which is a problem for the entire globe. It changes the weather so fast that animals cannot adapt or breed properly."
